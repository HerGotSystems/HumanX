# HumanX Structure

HumanX is an umbrella system.

It is not only a claim board.

## Core layers

### 1. Belief Engine

Personal layer.

Maps:

- inherited beliefs
- contradiction pressure
- tribal identity load
- emotional attachment
- belief survivability
- reaction patterns

Output:

- belief profile
- contradiction map
- personal pressure graph

The result can optionally feed into Claim Engine.

---

### 2. Claim Engine

Public pressure-testing layer.

Claims are:

- defended with evidence
- attacked with pressure points
- tested for repeatability
- voted on by humans
- exported as AIP packets

Important principle:

Consensus is not truth.

Belief count and evidence strength are separate systems.

---

### 3. Evidence Vault

Reusable evidence infrastructure.

Evidence can support or pressure multiple claims.

Examples:

- documents
- scientific papers
- videos
- datasets
- photos
- measurements
- repeatability tests
- telemetry
- testimony

Evidence should become:

- reusable
- linkable
- source-trackable
- reliability-rated
- duplicate-clustered

---

### 4. Truths Layer

Truths are repeated statements, doctrines, slogans, inherited assumptions or public certainties.

Examples:

- cultural truths
- religious truths
- political truths
- scientific consensus truths
- family truths
- social truths
- institutional truths

A truth statement is NOT automatically factual.

HumanX separates:

- repetition
- certainty
- emotional weight
- evidence pressure
- repeatability

Truths can later become pressure-tested claims.

---

## HumanX Direction

HumanX is evolving toward:

```text
Belief
  ↓
Truth Statements
  ↓
Claims
  ↓
Evidence
  ↓
Pressure
  ↓
Repeatability
```

The system attempts to expose:

- why humans believe things
- why claims survive
- why claims collapse
- how repetition affects perception
- how certainty differs from proof
