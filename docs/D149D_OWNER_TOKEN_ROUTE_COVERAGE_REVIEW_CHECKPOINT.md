## Disposable Data Created

This checkpoint deliberately created **disposable, verification-only data**, not real user content:

- `snapshot_created: true` — a belief snapshot created specifically for this verification pass.
- `truth_promoted: true` — that snapshot was promoted to a truth, specifically to safely exercise `POST /api/belief-promote` without touching any real claim/truth content.
- `truth_archive_attempted: true` — an archive was attempted against that same disposable promoted truth, specifically to exercise `POST /api/my-humanx/archive` without archiving any real user data.

This data exists in production as a side effect of deliberately generating telemetry coverage. It is recorded here for transparency; no cleanup action was taken or verified as part of this checkpoint.

---

## Interpretation of `status_counts`

All 13 persisted records (after this pass) are `valid`; every other bucket (`secret_missing`/`missing`/`invalid`/`expired`/`uid_mismatch`) remains at 0. This is a clean signal across a now much more complete route set, but it remains a small, deliberately-generated sample from a single owner session, not organic traffic across varied conditions (different browsers, network timing, stale localStorage states, etc. — the exact scenarios D-148A's client hardening was designed to handle).

## Interpretation of `valid_ratio`

`valid_ratio: 1` both before and after — unchanged, and expected, since this entire sample was generated by one already-bootstrapped, already-working owner session. A `valid_ratio` of 1 from a deliberately-curated trigger pass says "the happy path works for every route," which is valuable and newly confirmed here, but it does not establish what the ratio looks like under organic conditions where boot-race timing, stale tokens, or other edge cases D-148A addressed could still produce a non-`valid` status occasionally.

---

## Remaining Enforcement Blockers

1. **Sample is deliberately generated, not organic.** All 13 records came from one verification session run by the owner. No conclusion about real-world adoption rates across different users, devices, or browser states can be drawn from this.
2. **Disposable test data now exists in production.** Not itself an enforcement blocker, but worth tracking — a snapshot, a promoted truth, and an archive action were created/attempted specifically for this verification.
3. **No time-distributed sampling yet.** All data points were generated in a single short session; there's still no signal about whether `status` distributions are stable over hours/days of real usage.
4. **Negative-status buckets remain entirely unobserved.** Zero `missing`/`invalid`/`expired`/`uid_mismatch` records exist anywhere in the persisted data to date — which is good news for the system's correctness, but also means there's no evidence yet of how the system behaves when those cases do occur in practice.

---

## Explicit Verdict: No Enforcement

**This review does not support enabling any enforcement, soft or hard.** All 8 routes now have at least one observed `valid` telemetry record — a genuine, confirmed improvement over D-149C's single-route coverage — but the underlying sample remains small and deliberately generated rather than organic. Consistent with every prior checkpoint since D-146A: the system remains fully advisory-only, `ownerTokenStatus()`'s result is not used to allow or reject any request, and nothing about this verification pass changes that.

## Recommended Next Patch

**Longer passive sampling, not soft-warning design yet.** With all 8 routes now confirmed reachable and instrumented correctly, the most valuable next step is to let real, organic traffic accumulate over a meaningful period (not another deliberately-triggered pass) and then review the resulting `status_counts`/`route_status_counts` for any naturally-occurring `missing`/`invalid`/`uid_mismatch` signal. That organic baseline — not this checkpoint's curated happy-path sample — is the actual prerequisite for designing a soft-warning mode or revisiting enforcement readiness.
